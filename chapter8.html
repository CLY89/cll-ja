1. What are you pointing at?

以下のシマヴォをこの節で説明する。 

poi	NOI	関係節を導く
ke'a	GOhA	
ku'o	KUhO

Let us think about the problem of コミュニケーション what it is that we are pointing at something. In Lojban, we can refer to what we are pointing at by using the pro-sumti ``ti" if it is nearby, or ``ta" if it is somewhat further away, or ``tu" if it is distant. (Pro-sumti are explained in full in Chapter7.)

However, even with the assistance of a pointing finger, or pointing lips, or whatever may be appropriate in the local culture, it is often hard for a listener to tell just what is being pointed at. Suppose one is pointing at a person (in particular, in the direction of his or her face), and says: 

1.1) ti cu barda
(これは大きい。)
What is the referent of ``ti"? Is it the person? Or perhaps it is the person's nose? Or even (for ``ti" can be plural as well as singular, and mean "these ones" as well as ``this one") the pores on the person's nose? ページ内リンクのテスト1.1。
